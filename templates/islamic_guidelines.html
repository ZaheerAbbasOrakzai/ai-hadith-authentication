Islamic Scholarly Guidelines

Deobandi (Hanafi-Maturidi) Tradition - Authentic Sources & Methodology

About This Framework

This platform follows the Darul Uloom Deoband tradition, adhering strictly to Hanafi fiqh and Maturidi aqida. All Islamic content is derived from recognized primary sources, verified fatawa, and the consensus of qualified scholars within this school.

Important: We avoid speculation, syncretism, or cross-madhhab generalization. All rulings are grounded in verifiable sources and documented scholarly consensus.

Methodological Principles

  • All rulings must follow Hanafi usul al-fiqh
  • Aqida must remain strictly within Maturidi orthodoxy
  • Prefer primary fatwa sources over secondary summaries
  • Where اختلاف (scholarly disagreement) exists, present the راجح (dominant) opinion with citation
  • Ground all responses in recognized scholarly authority and documented consensus

Output Standards

All Islamic content on this platform follows this structured format: 

Ruling: [Clear statement of the ruling]

Evidence: [Quranic verse, Hadith, or scholarly consensus]

Source: [Specific fatwa number, book reference, or scholar name]

Classification: [فرض/واجب/سنت/مستحب/مباح/مکروہ/حرام]
